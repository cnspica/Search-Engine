            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p>You can use <em>@name</em> syntax anywhere in your comment to reply to a specific user. This will notify that user in their <a href="http://blog.stackoverflow.com/2010/09/new-global-inbox/">global inbox</a>. There can also be <a href="http://blog.stackoverflow.com/2011/10/improved-global-email-notifications/">notification through email</a> if you set it up in the preferences found in your profile page. </p> 
             <h3>Who can be notified with this feature?</h3> 
             <ul> 
              <li><p>The author of the post.</p> <p>Note that the author of the post will <em>always</em> be notified of any new comment. You may still use it for clarity, if needed; however if only you and the author have commented on the post so far, the <em>@name</em> will be <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/97098/eeeeek-what-happened-to-my-salutation/97099#97099">automatically removed</a> from the beginning of the comment, as it adds no value.</p></li> 
              <li><p>Any user who has a visible (non-deleted) comment on the post.</p> <p>Note if a user comments on their own post and there is only one person who has previously commented, then that person is <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/82134/micro-refinement-to-notifications-for-comments-of-others-posts">also notified</a> if <em>@name</em> is not included.</p></li> 
              <li><p>Any user who has edited the post (does not include pending or rejected edit suggestions).</p></li> 
              <li><p>For questions: The moderator or gold badge holder who closed or reopened the question. Users who have closed or reopened a question <em>without</em> a binding close vote (i.e. without a gold tag badge or a moderator vote) cannot be notified.</p></li> 
              <li><p>For questions: any user who set a bounty on the question (current or expired)</p></li> 
             </ul> 
             <p>Keep in mind that the question and answers are all considered independently. For example, if Alice was the author of the question, then you cannot notify her by commenting on Bob's answer (unless Alice <em>also</em> participated in that answer). Similarly, you cannot notify Bob by commenting on Alice's question.</p> 
             <h3>Why do some names not appear in the auto-complete box?</h3> 
             <p>The auto-complete box can determine when an <em>@name</em> would not be necessary to trigger a notification and in that case will not include the user in the list.</p> 
             <p>Additionally, only users who have commented on the post are ever populated into the list. Editors and other users from the post's history will never appear there, even if they are able to be notified by typing manually.</p> 
             <h3>Can I notify more than one person at a time?</h3> 
             <p>No. Comments containing more than one <em>@name</em> <a href="http://meta.math.stackexchange.com/questions/2094/should-we-discourage-the-user-of-multiple-redundant-username-pings/2615#2615">are blocked</a> unless they contain a backtick <code>`</code>. In the latter case, only the first name mentioned using the <em>@name</em> syntax will be <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/2035/notify-user-when-their-name-is-mentioned-in-comment/35914#35914">notified</a>. For example, <em>@alice `@bob Hi!`</em> will notify Alice (if she has participated in that post), but not Bob.</p> 
             <p>An exception is the case when the first <em>@name</em> either matched nobody, or matched the post's author (and thus isn't necessary); in this case, the next <em>@name</em> will be checked.</p> 
             <h3>Can I change who gets notified after the comment is posted?</h3> 
             <p>When editing a comment within its limited editing period, if you change or add <em>@name</em>, <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/40989/will-edited-comments-reach-addressees/74781#74781">the notification may or may not reach the new recipient</a> depending on timing.</p> 
             <h3>Are there any special keywords for notifying users?</h3> 
             <p>No. Things like <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/52368/does-op-also-notify-the-op"><em>@op</em></a>, <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/37090/feature-request-downvoter-sends-a-notification-to-all-downvoters-for-your-post"><em>@downvoter</em></a>, or <em>@all</em> have no special meaning and will not trigger any notifications.</p> 
             <h3>How do the names get matched?</h3> 
             <ul> 
              <li><p>You must include <em>@name</em>, where <em>name</em> is a reasonable match to a user's <a href="http://chat.meta.stackoverflow.com/transcript/message/481203#481203">current display name</a> at the time the comment is submitted. You cannot use previous display names the target may have had.</p></li> 
              <li><p>The notification must begin with a space or be at the start of the comment. For example, you <a href="http://programmers.stackexchange.com/questions/39/whats-your-favourite-quote-about-programming/1079#comment-19850">cannot use markup</a> such as italics.</p></li> 
              <li><p>If the first word in the display name is at least three characters long, then there must be a starts-with, case-insensitive match of at least three characters in the display name. This means <em>@a</em> and <em>@ab</em> will never match anyone, <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/61960/allow-for-comment-reply-to-usernames-with-less-than-three-characters-in-first-wor/75809#75809">unless</a> a user uses a first word that is only two characters. Like: <em>@Jo</em> will notify Jo Miller, but not John, and <em>@B.</em> will notify B. Gates, but not B.Gates. If there are more than three characters in <em>@name</em>, then all given characters must match (neither <em>@alix</em> nor <em>@aliceinwonderland</em> will match user Alice).</p></li> 
              <li><p>Matching is performed in reverse chronological order, so if five people named John are participating, <em>@john</em> will match the most recent John. (Use the next rule to differentiate.)</p></li> 
              <li><p>Spaces are removed <em>from the display names</em> for matching purposes. So to match Peter Smith you may use <em>@pet</em>, <em>@peter</em>, <em>@peters</em>, or <em>@petersmith</em>. The last two are useful if Peter Jones is also participating, who can then be distinguished using <em>@peterj</em>. However, no spaces are allowed in the <em>@name</em> itself. Like to notify P Smith, one must use <em>@psm</em> or <em>@psmith</em>. (Here <em>@P Smith</em> would be handled as just <em>@P</em>, which is too short.) Single quotes, dots, dashes and underscores should <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/51911/the-dot-in-username-within-first-3-character-preventing-from-comment-replies/51913#51913">not</a> be removed.</p></li> 
              <li><p>Special characters <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/48968/peter-works-for-peter/49409#49409">are replaced</a> with their simple equivalent. To reply to Piëre you can use both <em>@piëre</em> and <em>@piere</em>. And to reply to Jörg you could use <em>@jorg</em>, <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/89051/joerg-works-for-jorg">but not</a> <em>@joerg</em>.</p></li> 
              <li><p>The notification only works for the display name that <a href="http://chat.meta.stackoverflow.com/transcript/message/481203#481203">is current</a> at the time the comment is submitted, not any previous username(s) the target may have had.</p></li> 
             </ul> 
             <h3>Does other punctuation affect the notification?</h3> 
             <p>The system does attempt to ignore most punctuation to allow for proper notifications, such as a single trailing dot, comma, or colon. The exact matching rules are not documented, but extensive tests performed on 30&nbsp;August 2011 indicate that the following algorithm is used:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li>Take the first word immediately following the <code>@</code>, where the word boundary is determined by a space, a character not valid in user names (such as <code>: , / ! ?</code>) or the end of the comment. </li> 
              <li>Remove one trailing dot <code>.</code> from the resulting string if applicable (and if the string has more than two characters). </li> 
              <li>If the remaining string ends in <code>'</code> or <code>'s</code>, remove that piece from the string. </li> 
              <li>The resulting string will be matched against the user name with spaces removed.</li> 
             </ul> 
             <p>Example: The comment ends with <code>@O'Conner's.)</code>. The word immediately following the <code>@</code> is <code>O'Conner's.</code> since <code>'</code> and <code>.</code> are valid in display names. Then the trailing dot is removed, and finally the <code>'s</code> is removed, resulting in <code>O'Conner</code>.</p> 
             <p>Some examples of supported notifications:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li><code>@name some text</code></li> 
              <li><code>@name: some text</code></li> 
              <li><code>@name. Some text</code></li> 
              <li><code>@name, some text</code></li> 
              <li><code>some text, @name</code></li> 
              <li><code>some text, @name, more text</code></li> 
              <li><code>Some text, @name.</code></li> 
              <li><code>This is mentioned in @name's comment.</code></li> 
              <li><code>@P.</code> for both P. Smith and P. Jones (whoever commented most recently), but not P.Smith nor P.Jones. Likewise, <code>@P. Smith</code> is handled as <code>@P.</code>, so also matches P. Smith or P. Jones.</li> 
              <li><code>@psm</code> or <code>@psmith</code> for P Smith</li> 
              <li><code>@peters</code> or <code>@peterj</code> for Peter Smith or Peter Jones respectively</li> 
              <li><code>@name...</code></li> 
             </ul> 
             <p>Examples that will <strong>not</strong> trigger notifications:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li><code>abc@name</code></li> 
              <li><code>*@name*</code></li> 
              <li><code>*@name:*</code></li> 
              <li><code>[@name](http://some-url)</code></li> 
              <li><code>@[name](http://some-url)</code></li> 
              <li><code>@P Smith</code></li> 
             </ul>
