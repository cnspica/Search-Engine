      <div class="about-intro"> 
       <h2 class="about-title">Welcome to Meta Stack Exchange</h2> 
       <img src="/content/Sites/stackexchangemeta/img/apple-touch-icon@2.png?v=c34d758b103d" class="site-icon"> 
       <div class="col-full"> 
        <p> </p>
        <div class="wiki-ph" data-placeholder="WikiPlaceholder-AboutThisSite"> 
         <div class="wiki-ph-content">
          <b>Meta Stack Exchange</b> is a question and answer site for meta-discussion of the Stack Exchange family of Q&amp;A websites. It's built and run 
          <i>by you</i> as part of the 
          <a href="http://stackexchange.com">Stack Exchange</a> network of Q&amp;A sites. With your help, we're working together to build a library of detailed answers to every question about the software that powers the Stack Exchange network.
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <p></p> 
        <p class="helper-text"> Meta is a little bit different from our main Q&amp;A sites. Here's how: </p> 
       </div> 
      </div> 
      <hr> 
      <div class="about-qa"> 
       <h2 class="about-title"> Ask questions about issues that affect <i>all</i> Stack Exchange sites </h2> 
       <div> 
        <p>The guidance on all our Q&amp;A sites explicitly disallows meta-discussion, to reduce clutter and noise. There's no chit-chat; just questions and answers.</p> 
        <p>In order to keep improving our sites, though, we need a space to talk about how the sites themselves work.</p> 
        <p>So each community has a meta to discuss site-specific issues, and Meta Stack Exchange is for conversations about problems and ideas that affect the whole network.</p> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-l"> 
        <p>This site is all about <b>getting answers</b>. It's not a discussion forum. There's no chit-chat.</p> 
        <div class="just-qa"> 
         <div class="just-questions"> 
          <p>Just questions...</p> 
          <div class="arrow-1"></div> 
         </div> 
         <div class="just-answers"> 
          <p>...and answers.</p> 
          <div class="arrow-2"></div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
        <div class="good-answers"> 
         <span class="vote-up-on fl">up vote</span> 
         <p>Good answers are voted up and <b>rise to the top</b>.</p> 
         <p class="helper-text">The best answers show up first so that they are always easy to find.</p> 
        </div> 
        <div class="accept-answers"> 
         <span class="vote-accepted-on fl">accept</span> 
         <p>The person who asked can mark one answer as "accepted".</p> 
         <p class="helper-text">Accepting doesn't mean it's the best answer, it just means that it worked for the person who asked.</p> 
        </div> 
       </div>

      <div class="about-offtopic"> 
       <h2 class="about-title">Be part of the community that decides how the sites work</h2> 
       <div class="col-l on-topic-text"> 
        <p>Any question that has to do with the Stack Exchange family of communities is fair game.</p> 
        <div class="arrow-4"></div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-r"> 
        <div class="on-topic-examples content-block"> 
         <p>Ask about...</p> 
         <div class="wiki-ph" data-placeholder="WikiPlaceholder-AboutOnTopic"> 
          <div class="wiki-ph-content">
           <ul> 
            <li>Specific issues with the software that powers the Stack Exchange <a href="http://stackexchange.com/sites">network</a></li> 
            <li>Real problems or questions that you’ve encountered using this software</li> 
           </ul> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-l off-topic-text"> 
        <p>While we discourage opinions on our main sites, questions here are likely to generate discussion and involve opinions. That's okay!</p> 
        <div class="arrow-4"></div> 
       </div> 
       <div class="col-r"> 
        <div class="off-topic-examples content-block"> 
         <p>However, you shouldn't ask about...</p> 
         <div class="wiki-ph" data-placeholder="WikiPlaceholder-AboutOffTopic"> 
          <div class="wiki-ph-content">
           <ul> 
            <li>Anything not directly related to the software that powers the Stack Exchange <a href="http://stackexchange.com/sites">network</a></li> 
            <li>Questions that require the expertise of a specific Stack Exchange community - these should be asked on that site's meta instead</li> 
           </ul> 
          </div> 
         </div> 
        </div> 
       </div> 
      </div>

      <div class="about-tags"> 
       <h2 class="about-title">Tags make it easy to find interesting questions</h2> 
       <div class="col-l"> 
        <p>All questions are <b>tagged</b> with their subject areas. Each can have up to 5 tags, since a question might be related to several subjects.</p> 
        <p>On meta sites, every question must contain one of the following tags: </p>
        <ul> 
         <li><a href="/questions/tagged/feature-request" class="post-tag required-tag" rel="tag">feature-request</a></li> 
         <li><a href="/questions/tagged/support" class="post-tag required-tag" rel="tag">support</a></li> 
         <li><a href="/questions/tagged/bug" class="post-tag required-tag" rel="tag">bug</a></li> 
         <li><a href="/questions/tagged/discussion" class="post-tag required-tag" rel="tag">discussion</a></li> 
        </ul> 
        <p></p> 
        <p> Tags with a red background (like <a href="/questions/tagged/featured" class="post-tag moderator-tag" rel="tag">featured</a> or <a href="/questions/tagged/status-completed" class="post-tag moderator-tag" rel="tag">status-completed</a>) can only be applied by a moderator or Stack Exchange employee. </p>

       <h2 class="about-title">Voting is different on meta sites</h2> 
       <div> 
        <p>Your Meta Stack Exchange account functions like an account on any other Stack Exchange Q&amp;A site: your reputation score goes up when others vote up your questions and answers, and you earn badges for your activity here.</p> 
        <p>For most posts, votes reflect the perceived usefulness: well-written, well-reasoned, and well-researched posts tend to get more attention and more upvotes.</p> 
        <p> Unlike normal Stack Exchange sites, meta invites the community to discuss, debate, and propose changes to the way the community itself behaves, as well as how the software works. <b>On posts tagged</b> <a href="/questions/tagged/feature-request" class="post-tag required-tag" rel="tag">feature-request</a>, voting can indicate agreement or disagreement with the proposed change rather than just the quality or usefulness of the post itself. </p> 
       </div> 
      </div>
