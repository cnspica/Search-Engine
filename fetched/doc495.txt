       <h3>A message from Stack Overflow</h3> 
       <h2 class="sectionTitle"> Careers has moved all job search features to <a href="http://stackoverflow.com/jobs?t=c&amp;r=home">Stack Overflow Jobs</a> </h2> 
       <p> Moving forward, all Careers users can use a Stack Overflow account to access their Careers job search data. This includes the CV, match preferences, favorites, settings, and message history. Careers will continue to operate as a sourcing and recruiting product, and employer account data will not be affected. </p> 
       <h2 class="sectionTitle"> Why we're making this change </h2> 
       <p> In 2009, Stack Overflow embarked on a mission to help all developers find jobs they love. As the Careers product grew, we learned that its position outside of Stack Overflow - at a distinct URL requiring unique account credentials and data from our users - caused a fragmented user experience for our developer community. By unifying our Stack Overflow developer services under a single account, we hope to create a more seamless experience and to better serve all developers in their search for jobs they love. <a href="http://meta.stackoverflow.com/questions/312452/careers-unificintegration-jobs-on-stack-overflow?rq=1">You can read more about this change on Meta Stack Overflow.</a> </p>
