           <td class="answercell"> 
            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p>There really isn't such a thing as approving one. </p> 
             <p>It can get implemented.</p> 
             <p>The SE team does this.</p> 
             <p>One of the community managers or the implementing developer will then slap a <a href="/questions/tagged/status-completed" class="post-tag moderator-tag" title="show questions tagged 'status-completed'" rel="tag">status-completed</a> onto it.</p> 
             <p>Essentially, all feature requests are on the radar of SE employees. Which of those get implemented depends on a number of things, most of which are not public. We only hear from them again when the FR is either declined or implemented, or sometimes <a href="/questions/tagged/status-planned" class="post-tag moderator-tag" title="show questions tagged 'status-planned'" rel="tag">status-planned</a> or <a href="/questions/tagged/status-review" class="post-tag moderator-tag" title="show questions tagged 'status-review'" rel="tag">status-review</a>.</p>
