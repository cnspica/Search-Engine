  <summary type="html"> &lt;p&gt;&lt;a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/238493/update-the-date-when-a-question-is-migrated"&gt;This question&lt;/a&gt; requests that migrations get updated dates to match the time of their migration. That is not the feature I am asking for.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;I am asking that, &lt;strong&gt;in the case of migrations&lt;/strong&gt;, could the system be changed such that it uses the migration date as the "asked date" so that the question can be found more easily? - Yes, this is a subtle difference but I think it's an important one.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;I love the active view. Most of the time it's the only view I use. This is great because I see anything that's active, including recently migrated questions.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;I am not the only user on this network (imagine that)... &lt;a href="http://meta.ell.stackexchange.com/questions/3062/what-are-our-thoughts-on-how-elu-is-doing-at-migrating-to-ell?noredirect=1#comment7295_3062"&gt;some users&lt;/a&gt; only (or primarily) use the "newest" view. Some of these users are very valuable on a network and it's unfortunate that their primary choice of view means that they never see migrated questions... I'd argue that this also hurts the migrated question's chance of getting a good answer or insightful comments to help improve the question.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Because we use the "asked" date in all cases, these questions could be days and weeks down the list!&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Normally, this doesn't matter. Questions are asked, they get their chance at the top of the "newest" list, and then they slowly fade into the past. Migrations are the exception.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;hr&gt; &lt;p&gt;To address the concerns posed by &lt;a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/users/155160/adam"&gt;Adam&lt;/a&gt; in his &lt;a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/a/238501/284336"&gt;answer&lt;/a&gt;:&lt;/p&gt; &lt;blockquote&gt; &lt;p&gt;If, say, an older question were to be migrated, it'd be kind of weird to pretend that it was just asked today. &lt;/p&gt; &lt;/blockquote&gt; &lt;p&gt;In this case, we're not changing the asked date, so we're not pretending it was just asked today, we are, however allowing that it was, essentially, just asked today &lt;strong&gt;on this new site&lt;/strong&gt;.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;blockquote&gt; &lt;p&gt;Not to mention that we'd either have to adjust every single timestamp (for example, edits, answers, etc) as well or we'd live with with posts or edits that looked like they were made before the question was asked.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;/blockquote&gt; &lt;p&gt;&lt;strong&gt;No timestamps are harmed in the migrating of this question!&lt;/strong&gt; That is to say, we're not changing the timestamp of when the question was asked, so there's no incongruity between that and any of the other timestamps, so nothing needs to be changed and there's no confusion created.&lt;/p&gt; </summary>

  <summary type="html"> &lt;p&gt;I’ve labelled a few favourite tags here (and more on other sites). I appreciate them being highlighted on the question page.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Sometimes, however, I take breaks from Stack Exchange that can be anything between weeks and months. If any cool interesting question that uses my favourite tags happens to be asked during that time, I’m going to miss it. I don’t like that.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;But I also don’t want a fixed e-mail notification for the following reasons:&lt;/p&gt; &lt;ul&gt; &lt;li&gt;&lt;p&gt;When I’m active I don’t really need it, so I consider the e-mails spammy.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;&lt;p&gt;I actively want to stay away when I’m staying away, so I don’t want e-mail notifiers in that time.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;&lt;p&gt;I typically only check the e-mail account that is associated with SE once a day or less. I might well not have time to visit SE then and thus forget about the notification.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;&lt;p&gt;Stack Exchange and that e-mail will be open in different browser windows, so my brain does not do well connecting them.&lt;/p&gt;&lt;/li&gt; &lt;/ul&gt; &lt;p&gt;Rather, Stack Exchange has a beautiful feature that leads me &lt;em&gt;exactly&lt;/em&gt; to those places that seem interesting once I log on: The comment notification inbox.* That inbox also logs information about bounties (automatically) and chat responses after fifteen minutes. It is a beautiful place to send favourite question notifications to, because it is &lt;em&gt;right&lt;/em&gt; where I want it. With the added benefit of me actually wanting to spend time on SE whenever I log on and see that notification icon. (They don’t go bad even after three months.)&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Therefore I would like to request the feature that &lt;strong&gt;subscriptions to tags should be possible to the SE inbox rather than e-mail.&lt;/strong&gt;&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Additional clutter could be removed by an intelligent system that realises if I opened the question within 15 minutes of it being posted and then discards it from the notifications list. This already happens with chat replies and other users’ edits to one’s posts. Furthermore, it knows who &lt;em&gt;I&lt;/em&gt; am and doesn’t need to send me notifications of my own questions.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;hr&gt; &lt;p&gt;Notes:&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;*: Feel free to suggest a better name …&lt;/p&gt; </summary>

For example, on Movies &amp;amp; TV, we have tags for film titles, Sci-Fi and Fantasy has tags for titles, authors, etc… Anime &amp;amp; Manga is similar. &lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Occasionally, on M&amp;amp;TV, we get users (usually 1 rep) who use generic-looking tags like &lt;a href="/questions/tagged/aliens" class="post-tag" title="show questions tagged &amp;#39;aliens&amp;#39;" rel="tag"&gt;aliens&lt;/a&gt; to ask questions about… well… aliens in films… but the Aliens tag, as the tag wiki notes, is for questions about the film &lt;em&gt;Aliens&lt;/em&gt;. The tags already have the country name on them, so all that is needed to set them apart visibly is a different color of tag (one color for all country tags, not one color per country). This would make all of the country tags on a site more visible while not interfering or watering down Stack Exchange's ability to sell the icons to interested parties.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;hr&gt; &lt;p&gt;What I would like to propose is a specialized type of tag denoted with a different color that allows sites to do a couple of things:&lt;/p&gt; &lt;ul&gt; &lt;li&gt;Makes it obvious that the tag is not for general use, particularly when the tag looks like it's generic (see the aliens example above).&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;Makes the tag stand out on a question as a form of classification.&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;As on Meta, these tags would have precedence over other tags and be listed first to make them more obvious.&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;As with the tags on meta, they would not be exempt from the five tag limit.&lt;/li&gt; &lt;li&gt;Potentially, this &lt;strong&gt;could&lt;/strong&gt; allow a site to have more than one tag with the same name but that's not completely necessary. For established sites, this will be quite an endeavor at the outset as there are likely hundreds of tags that will need to be classified.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;As one would expect, this is something that should be implemented on a per-site basis and be something that the site has to show a particular need for and community support of before it is allowed.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;As a note, on M&amp;amp;TV we have recommended a plan to set film and TV tags apart by including the year of first release in the tag but this plan is on hold due to the issue with film/show title length often conflicting with the &lt;a href="//meta.stackexchange.com/questions/209248/increase-limit-on-tag-length" rel="nofollow"&gt;25-character limit for tags&lt;/a&gt;.&lt;/p&gt; &lt;p&gt;Note, I &lt;strong&gt;am not&lt;/strong&gt; asking to &lt;a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/78849/allow-custom-colorization-of-tags"&gt;Allow custom colorization of tags&lt;/a&gt;, which relates to a user-setting to mark favorite tags.&lt;/p&gt; </summary>
