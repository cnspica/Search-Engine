            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p><a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Wullf_Yularen">Wullf Yularen</a></p> 
             <blockquote> 
              <p>Wullf Yularen was an admiral for the Galactic Republic during the Clone Wars and later a colonel in the Galactic Empire's Imperial Security Bureau. He was eventually promoted to Deputy Director of the Naval Intelligence Agency. Yularen was one of the officers assigned to the Death Star.</p> 
             </blockquote> 
             <p>....</p> 
             <blockquote> 
              <p><strong>Behind the scenes</strong></p> 
              <p>Wullf Yularen first appeared as an unnamed background character in Star Wars: Episode IV A New Hope, played by Robert Clarke, and he was given the name Wullf Yularen by the Star Wars Legends card game Star Wars Customizable Card Game. Yularen would later be adopted by the Star Wars: The Clone Wars television series as a recurring character.</p> 
             </blockquote> 
             <p>I haven't found any explanation of why he's wearing a different uniform, but it might be tied to his position as Deputy Director of Naval Intelligence much as with the <a href="http://cdn.uniforms-4u.com/pd/5291/big-u-customizable-us-navy-service-dress-white-military-uniform-15821.png">United State "Full Whites" naval uniform</a>. Different division, different uniform. You can see him with someone else with the same uniform <a href="http://vignette3.wikia.nocookie.net/starwars/images/c/ce/Isbelevator.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/640?cb=20111104223649">here</a> from <em>A New Hope</em>. Out of universe, the real answer is probably closer to why there was <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Lak_Sivrak">a wolf-man</a> and <a href="http://scifi.stackexchange.com/questions/80002/what-evidence-is-there-that-proves-disproves-there-was-a-gemini-earth-astronaut">a person in a recognizable Gemini suit</a>. Low budget, so they grabbed what costumes they could.</p>

           <td class="answercell"> 
            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p>As Fuzzyboots has correctly indicated, this individual is Admiral <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Wullf_Yularen" rel="nofollow">Wullf Yularen</a>, later confirmed to be the Deputy Director of the Empire's Naval Intelligence Agency, the Imperial Security Bureau (ISB).</p> 
             <p>It would appears that this uniform (white, rather than olive or grey) denotes that one is a member of this agency, as can be seen with other ISB agents in both Legends and <a href="http://starwars.wikia.com/wiki/Alecia_Beck" rel="nofollow">Nu-Canon</a> sources.</p> 
             <p><a href="http://i.stack.imgur.com/Cef54.png" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/Cef54.png" alt="enter image description here"></a></p> 
             <p>Interestingly, the decision that this uniform should denote a <em>specific</em> branch of the Imperial Service itself seems to be a retcon. In other media such as the Thrawn comics below, the white uniform appears is worn by two Grand Admirals (Thrawn and Takel), neither of who has any obvious connection to the ISB.</p> 
             <p><a href="http://i.stack.imgur.com/DbVUb.jpg" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/DbVUb.jpg" alt="enter image description here"></a></p>
