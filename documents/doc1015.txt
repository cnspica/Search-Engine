             <h2>Why have Community Wiki posts?</h2> 
             <p>One of the goals of the website is to be a continually evolving source of good information. Community wiki posts help enhance the <em>wiki</em> aspect of the site. </p> 
             <p>For more information about the proper use of community wiki, see <a href="http://blog.stackoverflow.com/2011/08/the-future-of-community-wiki/">The Future of Community Wiki</a>.</p> 
             <h2>How do Community Wiki posts work?</h2> 
             <p>Community wiki posts work by partly transferring ownership of the post from the original author to the community. They make the post easier to edit and maintain by a wider group of users, but they do not contribute to any user's reputation. </p> 
             <p>Some of the key features are:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li>A much lower reputation (<strong>100</strong> instead of <strong>2000</strong>) is needed to freely edit a community wiki post. <sup>1</sup></li> 
              <li>Voting on a community wiki post (up or down) does not affect any user's reputation.</li> 
              <li>Accepting an answer marked as community wiki <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/a/145935/152859">does not affect its original author's reputation</a>.</li> 
              <li>Bounties awarded to answer marked as community wiki give reputation as usual.</li> 
              <li>The original author still gets badges based on community posts.</li> 
              <li>The original author keeps the reputation gained (or lost) before their post entered community mode.</li> 
              <li>The original author still receives notifications for edits or comments on the post.</li> 
              <li>The user listed in the author box need not be the original author. Rather, it is the user with the <a href="http://blog.codinghorror.com/mixing-oil-and-water-authorship-in-a-wiki-world/" rel="nofollow">highest percentage of authorship</a> in the final revision, determined by the number of lines inserted or deleted, with a bonus (factor of 2) for the original author. </li> 
             </ul> 
             <h2>How does a post become a Community Wiki post?</h2> 
             <p>There are only three ways that a post becomes community wiki.</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li><p>The <strong>answer</strong>'s author checks the <em>community wiki</em> checkbox when composing or editing the answer. Note that this checkbox isn't available to new users. The checkbox also is not available if the question is already a community wiki. <sup>2</sup></p></li> 
              <li><p>A moderator has reason to believe that the post serves better in community wiki mode - if you believe your post <a href="http://blog.stackoverflow.com/2011/08/the-future-of-community-wiki/">should be converted</a> to a community wiki, you may <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/392/should-the-community-wiki-police-be-shut-down/7183#7183">flag it for moderator attention</a>.</p></li> 
              <li><p>An answer posted to a community wiki question will also be community wiki. In the past, questions could be made community wiki by their authors or by certain automatic triggers but <a href="https://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/227290/stop-using-community-wiki-as-a-reputation-denial-mechanism/228940#228940">now the only means is by a moderator converting it</a> to community wiki. When a moderator converts a question to community wiki, all existing answers will also be converted in addition to converting future answers.</p></li> 
             </ul> 
             <h2>How can the Community Wiki status be removed from a post?</h2> 
             <p><a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/2974/is-there-a-way-to-remove-community-wiki-status/83373#83373">Moderators can remove it, if necessary.</a></p> 
             <h2>Miscellaneous</h2> 
             <ul> 
              <li><p>Rollbacks cannot be used to remove community wiki status.</p></li> 
              <li><p>The Community User<sup>3</sup> might appear as the last editor of a question, even a non-wiki question. This happens when it randomly pokes old unanswered questions so they get some attention.</p></li> 
             </ul> 
             <p><sup>1</sup> The original author of a community wiki post can <em>always</em> edit it, even if he or she has &lt; 100 reputation. Other edits made by users with less than 100 reputation must be reviewed in the usual way before being applied.</p> 
             <p><sup>2</sup> Since <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/a/67582/152859">October 2010</a>, there is no <em>community wiki</em> checkbox when asking a question.</p> 
             <p><sup>3</sup> See <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/questions/19738/who-is-the-community-user">Who is the Community User?</a>, or read the <a href="http://meta.stackexchange.com/users/-1">Community User’s profile</a>.</p>
