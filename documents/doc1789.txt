      <h1 class="blog-title"><a href="http://scifi.blogoverflow.com/2016/05/the-great-science-fictionfantasy-tv-showdown-results-and-round-two/">The Great Science-Fiction/Fantasy TV Showdown! – Results and Round Two</a></h1> 
      <div class="meta-info">
       <span class="postedDate">2016-05-02</span> by 
       <a href="http://scifi.blogoverflow.com/author/rogue-jedi/" title="Posts by Rogue Jedi" rel="author">Rogue Jedi</a>. 
       <a href="http://scifi.blogoverflow.com/2016/05/the-great-science-fictionfantasy-tv-showdown-results-and-round-two/#comments">2 comments</a> 
      </div> 
      <div class="post-text"> 
       <div class="tweetthis" style="text-align:left;">
        <p> <a target="_blank" rel="nofollow" class="tt" href="http://twitter.com/share?url=http://scifi.blogoverflow.com/2016/05/the-great-science-fictionfantasy-tv-showdown-results-and-round-two/&amp;text=The+Great+Science-Fiction%2FFantasy+TV+Showdown%21+%E2%80%93+Results+and+Round+Two&amp;via=StackSciFi&amp;related=richardxthripp%2Ctweetthisplugin" title="Post to Twitter"><img class="nothumb" src="http://scifi.blogoverflow.com/wp-content/plugins/tweet-this/icons/en/twitter/tt-twitter-micro3.png" alt="Post to Twitter"></a></p>
       </div>
       <p>First off, I’d like to thank everyone who’s helped me with this, including, but not limited to everyone who helped choose shows, everyone who voted and predicted, &nbsp;<a href="http://scifi.stackexchange.com/users/1148/jack-b-nimble" target="_blank">Jack B Nimble</a> for all the blog stuff and especially,&nbsp;<a href="http://scifi.stackexchange.com/users/31394/rand-althor" target="_blank">Rand al’Thor</a>, for helping me, giving positive feedback and defending this entire event multiple times.</p> 
       <p>And now, the results! I’m happy to write that 29 members voted and 19 predicted. The bold numbers represent how many people voted for each series.</p> 
       <ol> 
        <li><em>Star Trek: The Original Series</em>&nbsp;went boldly where no show had gone before and lost to <strong>Star Trek: The Next Generation</strong>.<strong>&nbsp; 17/3</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Futurama</strong> beat <em>Rick and Morty</em>. <strong>17/3</strong></li> 
        <li>Adventure Time just barely lost to&nbsp;<strong>MLP: Friendship is Magic</strong>. <strong>9/8</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Buffy the Vampire Slayer</strong>&nbsp;slew&nbsp;<em>Angel</em>. <strong>12/8</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Nu-Who</strong>&nbsp;beat Classic <em>Doctor Who</em>&nbsp;so much it couldn’t even regenerate. <strong>20/2</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<em>The Legend of Korra&nbsp;</em>couldn’t redirect <strong>Avatar: The Last Airbender</strong>‘s lightning. <strong>11/4</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<em>LOST&nbsp;</em>lost. <strong>The X-Files</strong>&nbsp;won. &nbsp;<strong>15/4</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Batman: The Animated Series</strong>&nbsp;beat Classic <em>Batman</em>&nbsp;like it&nbsp;was the Joker’s henchman. <strong>11/4</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;And the twist is that <strong>The Twilight Zone</strong>&nbsp;beat <em>The Outer Limits</em>. <strong>11/5</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;“This is a rebellion, isn’t it? I <del>rebel</del> lose to <strong>Star Wars: The Clone Wars</strong>“.&nbsp;<strong>12/5</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<em>TMNT (2012) </em>was dishonorable defeated by&nbsp;<strong>TMNT (Original)</strong>.&nbsp;<strong>12/5</strong></li> 
        <li>“When you play the <b>Game of Thrones</b>, you either win, or you die.” Let’s just say they didn’t die to&nbsp;<em>The Walking Dead.</em>&nbsp;<strong>9/7</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Babylon 5</strong> wasn’t the one who only got 5 votes. That was&nbsp;<em>Battlestar Galactica </em>(2004).&nbsp;<strong>10/5</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>The Flash</strong> just barely outran <em>Arrow</em>. <strong>9/8</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Justice League Unlimited</strong>&nbsp;apparently has unlimited appeal, compared to&nbsp;<em>The Spectacular&nbsp;Spider-Man</em>.&nbsp;<strong>10/1</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Farscape</strong> beat&nbsp;<em>Red Dwarf</em>.&nbsp;12/8</li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Daredevil</strong>&nbsp;beat&nbsp;<em>Marvel’s Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D.</em>&nbsp;Blindfolded.<strong>&nbsp;12/5</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Supernatural</strong>&nbsp;superbeat <i>Once Upon a Time.&nbsp;</i>10/4</li> 
        <li><strong>Stargate: SG-1</strong> beat&nbsp;<em>The Sarah Conner Chronicles</em>&nbsp;across all of time and space.<strong> 14/4</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy</strong>&nbsp;defeated <em>The Incredible Hulk.</em> He’s presumably quite angry now.&nbsp;<strong>10/6</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<em>Gravity Falls</em> gravity fell.&nbsp;<strong>Transformers</strong> won. <strong>9/6</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Sabrina the Teenage Witch&nbsp;</strong>beat&nbsp;<em>ALF</em>, therefore answering the age old question “who’s more popular, a magical teenager girl, or a minuscule alien who enjoys consuming cats?”<strong>&nbsp;12/7</strong></li> 
        <li>&nbsp;<strong>Quantum Leap&nbsp;</strong>quantum leapt right over <em>Torchwood.</em>&nbsp;<strong>14/8</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Firefly</strong> kept flying right past&nbsp;<em>Fringe.&nbsp;</em>&nbsp;<strong>21/5</strong></li> 
       </ol> 
       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
       <p>Here are the prediction recaps. Bold means the show is still in the running.</p> 
       <ul> 
        <li><strong>Star Trek: The Next Generation</strong>:<em>&nbsp;</em><strong>4</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Firefly</strong>: <strong>3</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Nu-Who</strong>:&nbsp;<strong>&nbsp;3</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>My Little Pony: Friendship is Magic</strong>:&nbsp;<strong>2</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Daredevil</strong>:<strong> 2</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Game of Thrones</strong>: <strong>2</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Batman: The Animated Series</strong>: <strong>2</strong></li> 
        <li><strong>Buffy the Vampire Slayer</strong>: <strong>1</strong></li> 
        <li>Star Trek: The Original Series: 1</li> 
       </ul> 
       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
       <p>And now, on to The Great Science-Fiction/Fantasy TV Showdown, Round Two!</p> 
       <p>After thinking about it for a long time, I’ve decided to arrange the surviving shows in an attempt to prevent steam-rolling. This may not not be entirely “fair” but I argue it gives better data on what shows are more popular among our user-base. For example, everyone knows that&nbsp;<em>Firefly&nbsp;</em>is far more popular than&nbsp;<em>Once Upon a Time</em>, but as of now, we don’t really know whether&nbsp;<em>Supernatural</em>&nbsp;or&nbsp;<em>Sabrina the Teenage Witch&nbsp;</em>are more popular.</p> 
       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
       <ol> 
        <li><strong><strong>The Heavy Contenders #1:&nbsp;</strong></strong><em>Star Trek: The Next Generation</em> and<b>&nbsp;</b><em>Nu-Who</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>The Heavy Contenders #2:</strong></strong><em>&nbsp;Game of Thrones</em> and<b>&nbsp;</b><em>Firefly</em></li> 
        <li><strong>Dave Filoni:</strong><em>&nbsp;Star Wars: The Clone Wars </em>and<em>&nbsp;Avatar: The Last Airbender</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>DC Animated:</strong></strong><em>&nbsp;Justice League Unlimited </em>and<em>&nbsp;Batman: The Animated Series</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>Marvel VS DC:&nbsp;</strong></strong><em>The Flash </em>and<em>&nbsp;Daredevil</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>Science Fiction with&nbsp;Numbers in their Names:</strong></strong><em>&nbsp;Babylon 5 </em>and<em>&nbsp;Stargate: SG-1</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>#80’s:&nbsp;</strong></strong><em>Transformers </em>(Original) and<em>&nbsp;TMNT </em>(Original)</li> 
        <li><strong><strong>Animation Across the Nations, 2.0 Edition:&nbsp;</strong></strong><em>Futurama </em>and<em>&nbsp;MLP: Friendship is Magic</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>Old School:&nbsp;</strong></strong><em>The Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy </em>(Series) and<em>&nbsp;Quantum Leap</em></li> 
        <li><strong>90’s and Beyond:&nbsp;</strong><em>Buffy the Vampire Slayer </em>and<em>&nbsp;The X-Files</em></li> 
        <li><strong><strong>Left-Overs (Part 1):</strong></strong><em>&nbsp;Farscape </em>and<em>&nbsp;The Twilight Zone </em>(Original)</li> 
        <li><strong><strong>Left-Overs (Part 2):&nbsp;</strong></strong><em>Sabrina the Teenage Witch </em>and<em>&nbsp;Supernatural</em></li> 
       </ol> 
       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
       <p>It’s too late to predict, but you can still vote for Round Two! Just click <a href="http://meta.scifi.stackexchange.com/questions/9546/the-great-science-fiction-fantasy-tv-showdown-round-2/9547#9547">here</a>.</p> 
       <p>&nbsp;</p> 
       <p>Remember, you can also chat about all aspects of the competition <a href="http://chat.stackexchange.com/rooms/38884/the-great-science-fiction-fantasy-tv-showdown-discussion-room">here</a>!</p>
