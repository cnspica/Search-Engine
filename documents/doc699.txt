            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p>Douglas Adams' American publishers <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life,_the_Universe_and_Everything#Censorship">forced him to change some of the text</a> in <em>Life, the Universe and Everything</em>: "Asshole" became "kneebiter", "shit" became "swut", and most bizarre and hilarious of all, <a href="http://scifi.stackexchange.com/questions/83573/why-is-belgium-the-rudest-word-in-hitchikers-guide-to-the-galaxy">"fuck" became "Belgium".</a> </p> 
             <h3>Has anyone associated with the US publisher (<a href="http://crownpublishing.com/imprint/harmony-books/">Harmony/Crown</a>/<a href="http://www.randomhousebooks.com/">Random House</a>) ever explained why they made Adams change the text?</h3>

             <h2>According to Adams, because kids were reading it</h2> 
             <p>As recounted in <em><a href="https://books.google.com/books?id=OdiQCwAAQBAJ&amp;pg=PT71&amp;lpg=PT71&amp;dq=hitchhiker%27s%20guide%20belgium%20america&amp;source=bl&amp;ots=rbaVCK-5bU&amp;sig=RkPTET3Ws-UO4-VO6lqqMky51V4&amp;hl=en&amp;sa=X&amp;ved=0ahUKEwjv-oKRsNbMAhVB2SYKHSlXA9YQ6AEIUTAI#v=onepage&amp;q=hitchhiker's%20guide%20belgium%20america&amp;f=false">Hitchhiker's Guide</a></em>, by M.J. Simpson:</p> 
             <blockquote> 
              <p>"I think what happened was my U.S. publisher was unhappy about using the word "fuck" because of the number of kids who read the books," explained Adams later. "I was a bit frustrated by that, but then it gave me an idea and I put in the whole Belgium piece."</p> 
             </blockquote> 
             <p>Of course, this was merely the author's impression of what his publishers wanted, not a statement from the publisher itself. </p> 
             <p>I was unable to find any direct statement from the publishers. </p>
