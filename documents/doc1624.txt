            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p>She is laundering money for criminals, either knowingly or unknowingly.</p> 
             <p>There are lots of ways to make fraudulent money transfers like credit card fraud, direct debit fraud or online banking trojans. Unfortunately (for the criminals) banks usually reverse such transfers when informed by their customers and inform the police, so criminals can not directly wire these transfers into their personal accounts.</p> 
             <p>That's where so-called "mules" come in. Mules are people who are hired by criminals to receive such dirty money and move it to a different account controlled by the criminals. When the transfer gets reversed, two things happen:</p> 
             <ol> 
              <li>The transfer from victim to mule gets reversed, but not the transfer from mule to criminal, so the mule loses their money while the criminal keeps theirs. That means the person who is the actual victim in the scheme is the mule.</li> 
              <li>Law enforcement gets suspicious of the mule and might knock on their door.</li> 
             </ol> 
             <p>Your "friend" is either one of these mules or in the business of recruiting mules.</p>
