             <p>I resorted to listing a selection of countries using as strategy:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li>List at least the countries from which my passport contains a stamp.</li> 
              <li>Add logic neighboring countries until reaching the limit.</li> 
             </ul> 
             <p>When I was searching around for a definitive answer, I observed two things:</p> 
             <ul> 
              <li>Everyone in this situation appears to resort to a similar strategy.</li> 
              <li>I didn't find any evidence of people running into problems due to this field.</li> 
             </ul> 
             <p>Which leads me to conclude that you probably shouldn't overthink it. But a definitive answer from official source would be nice, and I'm sure you will not accept anything less as the correct answer.</p>

            <div class="post-text" itemprop="text"> 
             <p>In the next step, after the page you have the problem at, there is a step for uploading a scan of your passport in <em>PDF format</em>. In that step, <strong>upload a 2-page PDF</strong>. The first page can have the scan of your passport. The second page of the PDF can have <strong>a letter explaining</strong> the issue and naming all the countries.</p> 
             <p><a href="http://i.stack.imgur.com/bCSfx.png" rel="nofollow"><img src="http://i.stack.imgur.com/bCSfx.png" alt="enter image description here"></a></p> 
             <p>In the previous page of the application, in the box for countries visited, include a reference to this uploaded page. I also recommend that you contact the help desk to make sure that this approach is alright. They may even give you another recommendation. </p> 
             <p>You can reach them at <code>+91 11243-00666</code> and indiatvoa@gov.in.</p> 
             <p>I had the same problem while applying for a regular visa (not e-visa) a couple of years ago, and I got an official response from the company the Indian embassy had outsourced the visa application process to in Australia.</p> 
             <p>I asked:</p> 
             <blockquote> 
              <p>In the "Countries visited in the last 10 years" section, there is not enough space for all the countries I have visited. What should I do about this?</p> 
             </blockquote> 
             <p>The reply was:</p> 
             <blockquote> 
              <p>You may provide a covering letter with the details of countries.</p> 
             </blockquote> 
             <p>The time when I got this answer was not the first time I had the problem. Actually, I've been in this situation all seven times I've applied for a visa to India. Sometimes I've written a supplementary letter with the countries, sometimes I've used abbreviations in the box (US, UK, DK, SL, etc.) I've always gotten the visa, and have never had any questions asked regarding that particular section.</p> 
             <p>Although it's extremely unlikely that the person processing your application will even notice or know if you leave out a bunch of countries, I'm not personally inclined to do that. I don't like taking such risks at all. Moreover, going to extra lengths to be truthful in your application gives a good impression to the person processing it. This is especially helpful if one is in a situation where there's a thin line between getting accepted or rejected. (I'm not suggesting this is the case for OP, I'm more talking in general to other people who may have the same problem in the future as there are likely a lot of people who will indeed have this problem.)</p> 
             <p>I'd also like to add that from my experience of several instances of applying for visas to India, I've found that it's better to get every single detail right. It's not always so, but in some cases a small detail being wrong can cause problems. Personally, I always proof read my applications once or twice before submitting them.</p> 
             <p><strong>TL;DR: Make a 2-page PDF containing both your passport and a letter regarding the countries and upload it in the passport section. Make every single detail correct.</strong></p>
